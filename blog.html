The Evolution of Indian Cinema: From Silent Era to Digital Revolution

Indian cinema's evolution spans over a century, transforming from silent films to digital masterpieces. Beginning with Dadasaheb Phalke's "Raja Harishchandra" in 1913, the industry progressed through distinct phases: the silent era's mythological focus, the golden age's parallel cinema movement, and the technical revolution of the 70s-90s. The digital age brought democratized filmmaking through CGI, advanced post-production, and streaming platforms. Key milestones include India's first talkie "Alam Ara" (1931) and first digital film "Fiza" (2001). As emerging technologies like VR and AI emerge, Indian cinema continues blending traditional storytelling with modern innovation while maintaining its cultural identity.
